# MLS Fellow Certification

**Level:** Expert | **Prerequisites:** MLS Professional + 2 years experience | **Review Duration:** 6 months

## Overview

The MLS Fellow certification recognizes deep expertise and significant contributions to the mask-lock ecosystem. This is a peer-reviewed credential, not an exam.

## Requirements

### Portfolio Submission

Candidates submit a portfolio demonstrating mastery across three or more areas:

1. **Original chip design** — A novel mask-lock chip that went to tape-out or advanced FPGA prototype
2. **Toolchain contribution** — Merged PRs to CLAWC, MLS spec, or reference designs
3. **Research publication** — Paper at a recognized venue (ISSCC, DAC, MICRO, VLSI, DATE, etc.)
4. **Open-source leadership** — Maintainer of an MLS ecosystem project with active community
5. **Education impact** — Developed curriculum, taught courses, mentored 5+ engineers to Associate/Professional

### Peer Review

- Portfolio reviewed by 3 existing MLS Fellows
- 30-minute technical interview with review panel
- Panel evaluates: technical depth, community impact, innovation

### Contribution Examples

- Designed a new form factor reference implementation
- Invented a novel ternary quantization technique
- Built a production mask-lock chip that shipped in a product
- Created tooling adopted by 100+ users
- Published foundational research cited by 10+ papers

## Fellow Privileges

- Voting member of MLS Standards Committee
- Keynote invitation at MLS annual conference
- Co-author on MLS specification updates
- Named in MLS Hall of Engineers
- Access to pre-release silicon for evaluation
- Annual Fellow summit (in-person)

## Application Process

1. Submit portfolio via GitHub (template provided)
2. Initial screening by program committee (2 weeks)
3. Full review by 3 Fellows (4 weeks)
4. Technical interview (1 hour)
5. Decision communicated within 6 months of submission

## Renewal

- Lifetime certification
- Fellows are expected to remain active in the community
- Inactive Fellows (no contributions for 3+ years) move to Emeritus status
